Screw pile basics

How they actually work

The foundation method, in plain English.

What are screw piles?

Screw piles are steel shafts with one or more welded helical plates, rotated into the ground using a drive motor on an excavator. The helices pull the pile down to the load-bearing depth specified by the engineer. Also called helical piles or helical piers. Engineered and installed to AS2159, the Australian Standard for piling design and installation.

How does a screw pile actually carry load?

The helices act like a giant screw thread, transferring vertical load from the structure into stable load-bearing soil below the active surface zone. Install torque is recorded on each pile and correlated to load capacity per the engineer's design - the higher the torque at the design depth, the higher the load the pile can carry.

What's the difference between a screw pile and a helical pier?

They're the same thing. Different industries and different generations of contractors use different names - screw pile is the more common Australian and UK term, helical pier is more common in the US. Same engineering, same install method, same Australian Standard.

How long have screw piles been used in Victoria?

Screw piles have been used in Australia for over 30 years, and across most of the world for over a century. Total Piling has been installing them across Victoria for 20 years specifically. Once a niche product, they're now standard practice on residential builds, civil sites, and government projects.

Installation & timeline

What an install actually looks like

How long, how disruptive, how it fits around the rest of your build.

How fast can you install screw piles for a residential build?

Most residential pad installs are done in a single day. We arrive on site with the right machine and pile stock, drive the piles to load-bearing depth specified by the engineer, and certify the result before we leave. Trades can start on top of them the same day - there is no curing time to wait for.

Do you need to dig a pit for each pile?

No. That's the main advantage over concrete piers. Screw piles are driven directly into the ground from the surface - no excavation pit, no formwork, no concrete pour, almost no spoil. The footprint of each pile is roughly the shaft diameter (76 to 114mm for standard sizes). Existing landscaping, paths, and lawns usually survive the install untouched.

Can you install around landscaping, existing trees, or tight access?

Yes. Our mini-excavator fleet handles tight-access work through standard doorways, side gates, and around existing structures. We work around mature trees by adjusting pile positions in agreement with your engineer. For very small access, hand-held drive motors are available for specialist jobs.

Soil & site

Will they work on your block?

The short answer for most Victorian soil profiles is yes - here's the detail.

Do screw piles work in reactive clay?

Yes, particularly well. Reactive clay is the dominant soil profile across most of Melbourne and a lot of Victoria - the clay swells in wet seasons and shrinks in dry, which is why shallow conventional footings often move. Screw piles pass through the active zone and anchor in stable soil below it, eliminating the seasonal movement problem at the source.

What about sandy or unstable soil?

Screw piles work in sand, silt, and most mixed soil profiles. In very loose sand or fill, the design depth goes deeper to find competent load-bearing material, and helix sizing is tuned accordingly. The soil report drives the engineering decision on size and depth - if the soil report shows the right profile is reachable, the piles work.

Can screw piles go through rock?

Soft rock and weathered material, yes. Hard rock is a different problem - if the design depth needs to anchor in or pass through dense rock, screw piles are not the right tool and a different foundation method is specified. We assess from the soil report and tell you up front whether the site is a fit.

Certification & documentation

What you get on completion

Compliance and paperwork, sized to the project.

How are screw piles certified to AS2159?

AS2159 is the Australian Standard that governs piling design, installation, and testing. We design to it from the engineer's specification, record install torque on every pile, perform load testing where the design requires it, and issue an AS2159 certificate of compliance at handover. The full documentation pack is what your structural engineer, builder, and council need for sign-off.

What documentation do I get with the install?

The standard handover pack includes the AS2159 certificate of compliance, per-pile install torque records, load test results where applicable, mill certificates for the steel, and as-built drawings showing pile positions. For government work, additional documentation is supplied to match the project's audit requirements.
