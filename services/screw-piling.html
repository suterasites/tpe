What it is

A modern foundation method, on Victorian sites for 20 years.

A screw pile is a steel shaft with one or more welded helical plates that gets rotated into the ground using a drive motor mounted on an excavator. The plates pull the pile down to the load-bearing depth specified by your engineer. Once it reaches the design torque, the pile is in - structurally complete, certified, and ready to take load the same day.

There is no concrete to pour, no curing window to wait through, no excavation pit, and almost no spoil to truck off site. For builders, that means tighter schedules and fewer surprises. For homeowners, that means foundations going in around a live home or developed site without the disruption of traditional methods. For civil and Tier 1 contractors, it means installs that hold up under engineering scrutiny and audit.

Common questions

Screw piling, answered

How fast can you install screw piles for a residential build?

Most residential pad installs are done in a single day. We arrive on site with the right machine and pile stock, drive the piles to the load-bearing depth specified by the engineer, and certify the result before we leave. Trades can start on top of them the same day - there is no curing time to wait for.

Do screw piles work in reactive clay or sandy soil?

Yes. Screw piles are particularly strong in reactive clay - which is common across Melbourne and large parts of Victoria - because they pass through the active zone and anchor in stable load-bearing soil below. They also work well in sand, silt, and most mixed profiles. The soil report drives the engineering decision on pile size and depth.

Can screw piles be removed or relocated later?

Yes. Screw piles are mechanically driven into the ground, not poured, so they can be unscrewed and removed at end of life or relocated for temporary structures. This is one of the things that sets them apart from concrete piers - they leave a far smaller footprint if the site ever needs to be returned to original condition.

How are screw piles certified?

Every install is engineered to AS2159 (the Australian Standard for piling design and installation). We record install torque on each pile against the engineer's design, perform load testing where specified, and hand over a certificate of compliance with the build documentation.

What is the difference between residential and commercial screw piles?

Same engineering principle, different scale. Residential piles are typically 76mm to 89mm shaft with single or double helix plates and rated 2,000 to 10,000+ kg each. Commercial and civil piles step up to 102mm and 114mm shafts with larger helices and significantly higher load capacity. We stock the full standard range and source custom sizes for Tier 1 work.
